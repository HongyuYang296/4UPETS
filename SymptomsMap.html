Symptoms Map

This is a map of the symptoms that dogs are likely to experience during a heat wave. Once you know about the Heat Index, or if you've already found out about the heat wave is coming, you can check your dog's body with the symptoms map below. Click on the dot for detailed symptoms.

Anxious

Dogs' senses may change when the barometric pressure is changing. When your dog is barking, cowering, hauling or guarding that may indicate that your dog is feeling unwell in the current environment.

X

Seizure

seizure occurs due to faulty electrical activity in the dog's brain which leads to a loss of control over the body. An overheated dog can suffer critical damage to his brain, heart, liver and nervous system. The brain swells, causing seizures, and lack of blood supply to the GI tract causes ulcers.

X

Red Gums

when dogs’ body temperature reaches 39.4°C or higher then you may see their gums become red or bright pink. This is a stress reaction when dogs try to mitigate their body temperature to the normal level by increasing the flow of blood to the dog’s extremities.

X

Salivation

Unlike the human body, dogs don’t sweat. Therefore, when you see your dogs salivating excessively that’s a sign of they feeling difficult to dissipate heat.

X

Sluggish

Extreme heat can let your pets look sluggish, this is the potential sign of heatwave related disease, and most owners may easily ignore it

X

Sharp rise of body temperature

The body temperature rises, which can generally rise to 41 to 42 degrees Celsius. If the dog cannot lower the body temperature quickly, it may cause serious brain damage or even death.

X

Collapsing

Typical collapse episodes begin 5 – 15 min after the onset of exercise and include disorientation, dull mentation or loss of focus; swaying, staggering and falling to the side; exaggerated lifting of each limb while walking and a choppy gait; scuffing of the rear and/or forelegs, and crossing of the legs when turning

X

Diarrhoea

One of the symptoms of heatstroke in dog is diarrhoea and bloody stools. Because of the rise in body temperature, the gastric mucosa will ulcerate, resulting in symptoms of diarrhoea and vomiting. When your pet has diarrhoea and bloody stools, it means that the degree of heatstroke is very serious
